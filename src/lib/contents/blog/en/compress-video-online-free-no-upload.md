## Two Ways to Compress: Presets vs. Target Size

### Quality Presets
Pick a preset and the tool handles the rest:

- **High Quality** — significantly smaller file, excellent visual quality. Best for archiving or sharing on large screens.
- **Balanced** — the sweet spot for most use cases. Sharp enough for any screen, small enough for most platforms. This is the recommended default.
- **Small File** — maximum compression. Some quality reduction, but perfectly watchable on phones and laptops. Best when file size is the only priority.

No technical knowledge needed. The compressor analyzes your video and calculates the optimal approach for the chosen preset automatically.

### Target File Size
Need the output under a specific limit? Enter the exact number:

- **16MB** for WhatsApp video messages
- **25MB** for Gmail and Outlook attachments
- **50MB** for a platform with a known upload cap
- **100MB** for a stricter hosting service

The tool works backward from your target — squeezing the best possible quality into the size you specified. No guesswork, no manual trial and error.

## Why Browser-Based Compression Is Better

Server-based tools have one unavoidable cost: **your video leaves your device**. Even with good privacy policies, your footage is temporarily stored on infrastructure you don't control.

Browser-based compression sidesteps this entirely. The video stays on your machine. Processing runs using your CPU and GPU through the browser's WebAssembly APIs — the same technology powering desktop tools like FFmpeg, running locally without any installation.

The result is faster, more private, and genuinely free.
